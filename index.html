You are not average.

Chemotherapy treatments are approved through large-scale clinical trials with an average acceptable toxicity response for a trial population. The problem with this approach is that you, as a person, donâ€™t have an average toxicity response. A major challenge for doctors is prescribing the right drug at the ideal dose to eliminate cancer without toxicity. While the drug may show efficacy against the cancer itself, its toxicity ultimately limits its use.



"Most medical treatments have been designed for the average patient — treatments can be very successful for some patients but not for others."

-The White House on the Precision Medicine Initiative

How do we fix this?

Leveraging recent advances in stem cell technology, Chemoguard models a patient's response to drugs using his or her own tissues. These cell cultures model the behavior of the patient's unique response to the drugs because they are derived from the patients' own cells.
